Mickey Madden: Biography

Michael Allen Madden, also known as Mickey Madden (born May 13, 1979) is an American musician, who is best known for being the former bassist in the pop rock band Maroon 5. Madden was born in Austin, Texas. He began playing in junior high school, playing in garages along with friends Jesse Carmichael (guitar/vocals) and Adam Levine (vocals/guitar). The bands Pearl Jam and Nirvana were among their influences. In 1994, with the addition of Ryan Dusick (drums), the band Kara's Flowers was formed. In July of 2020 he was arrested due to an alleged incident of domestic violence, which led him to leave the band and leaves Adam as the only original member of the band to not leave the band at some of time.
